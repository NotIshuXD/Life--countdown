Welcome to the Truth Behind Greenwashing

Many companies claim to be eco-friendly, but are they really? Let's dive into some real-life cases and uncover the truth.

Examples of Greenwashing

Fiji Water's 'Natural' Claim

Fiji Water has been marketed as 'natural' and 'untouched by man'. However, tests revealed the presence of microplastics and BPA, raising questions about its purity.

PepsiCo's Aquafina 'Pure' Water

PepsiCo's Aquafina brand claims to offer 'pure' water. However, a lawsuit alleged that the packaging contains BPA, contradicting the 'pure' claim.

Evian's 'Carbon Neutral' Label

Evian labeled its water as 'carbon neutral'. A lawsuit challenged this claim, arguing it misled consumers about the environmental impact.
