Carbon Offset Scam

Companies sell carbon offsets claiming to fight climate change, but investigations reveal many credits are fake or ineffective. Consumers end up paying for a false solution.

In 2024, Danone, the producer of Evian spring water, won the dismissal of a lawsuit regarding its 'carbon neutral' claims on its bottle labels. Consumers accused the company of misleading them into believing that the production process did not release carbon dioxide. Initially, Judge Nelson Roman permitted the class action to proceed, finding 'carbon neutral' ambiguous. However, upon reconsideration, he acknowledged that reasonable consumers could refer to the back label linking to Evian's website for a full explanation. Thus, he deemed Danone's claims 'technically true' with appropriate disclosures. It's a classic case of fine print vs. bold claims.
